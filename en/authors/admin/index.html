Roy Yali Samaniego

GIS Developer, Geospatial data geek, Geographer Engineer

San Marcos University- UNMSM

Roy Yali Samaniego (25) is a geography engineer at the National University of San Marcos; He has experience in the public and private sectors in the disciplines of geomatics and atmospheric sciences. He currently works as GIS developer at the Geological, Metallurgical Mining Institute of Peru - INGEMMET for GEOCATMIN projects and the Geoscientific Database in geoprocessing automation and publication of Services using different technology applied to Geographic Information Systems. As an engineer, he was also part of the “Automated Segmentation in the Rural Area” for the 2017 National Population and Housing Census project, as part of the National Institute of Statistics and Informatics - INEI. In science, he has carried out in the National Meteorology and Hydrology Service - SENAMHI in projects such as the “Methodological proposal for the delimitation of basin headwaters” and “Optimization of the National Hydrological Network”.
